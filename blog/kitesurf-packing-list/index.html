The Essential Packing List for your Kite Trip

Marine Tech Team • 5 Min Read

Packing for a kitesurfing trip is an art form. Pack too little, and you are stuck on the beach while everyone else rides. Pack too much, and you are paying excess baggage fees and lugging heavy coffins through airports. Cartagena has a unique tropical microclimate, so your packing list here will differ from a trip to Cape Town or Tarifa.

Whether you are renting gear from us or bringing your own, here is the checklist used by our pro team.

1. Technical Gear (If bringing your own)

If you choose to bring your own equipment, focus on the wind statistics. Cartagena is consistent but rarely "nuking" strong.

  • Kites: A 12m and a 9m are the holy grail here. If you are heavier (>90kg), consider a 14m/10m combo. If lighter (<60kg), an 8m/11m. You will rarely need a 7m or smaller unless it is the peak of February trade winds.
  • Board: A standard twin-tip works best. The water can be choppy in the afternoon, so a board with decent rocker helps smooth out the ride.
  • Harness: Bring your own. It fits you best.
  • Pump: If you can fit it, bring it. If not, we have pumps at the school, but having your own guarantees you aren't waiting in line.

Rental Tip: Airlines often charge $100+ USD each way for golf bags. Our rental packages start at competitive rates and include the latest Duotone gear. Do the math—it might be cheaper (and easier) to rent.

2. Sun Protection (Critical)

We cannot stress this enough: The Caribbean sun is relentless. You are close to the equator, and the reflection off the water doubles the UV exposure.

  • Zinc Stick / Sunscreen: Regular sunscreen washes off in 20 minutes. Bring heavy-duty Zinc (the colored stuff) for your face, nose, and lips.
  • Lycra / Rashguard: Do not kite shirtless for long sessions. A long-sleeve Lycra is mandatory to avoid ending your trip early with sunstroke.
  • Sunglasses with Leash: The glare is blinding. Polarized sunglasses are great, but ensure they have a secure strap, or the ocean will claim them.
  • Hat: A trucker hat helps on the beach, but a bucket hat with a chin strap is better for riding.

3. Clothing & Lifestyle

Cartagena is hot and humid. Leave the jeans and jackets at home.

  • Boardshorts / Bikinis: Bring at least 3 pairs. You will live in them.
  • Light Linen Shirts: For the evenings in the Old City. Cartagena has a dressy nightlife, but it needs to be breathable fabrics.
  • Flip Flops: Essential.
  • Dry Bag: Useful for boat trips (like our Downwind to Rosario Islands) to keep your phone and wallet dry.

4. First Aid & Misc

We have full first aid kits at Marine Tech, but a personal travel kit is wise.

  • Aqua Ear drops: To prevent "swimmer's ear" after long days in the water.
  • Waterproof Phone Case: If you want to take pictures, though we offer professional photography services.
  • Electrolytes: Powdered electrolytes to add to your water. Recovery is key.

What NOT to bring

  • Wetsuit: The water temperature averages 28°C (82°F). A wetsuit is overkill and will just make you overheat.
  • Huge Towels: Most hotels and our school provide towels. A microfiber travel towel saves space.

Forget something?

Don't worry. Cartagena is a major city. We have surf shops and pharmacies nearby. And remember, if you don't want to haul gear, you can book our Premium Rental Package which includes kite, board, bar, and harness.

Travel light, ride hard.
