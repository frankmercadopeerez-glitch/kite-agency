Is Kitesurfing Safe in Cartagena? A Naval Analysis

By Frank Mercado (Ex-Navy Officer) • 10 Min Read

As a former Navy Officer, I look at the ocean differently. Where others see a playground, I see variables: wind speed, current direction, obstacles, and exit strategies. One of the most common questions I receive from potential students is: "Is kitesurfing dangerous?"

The honest answer is: Kitesurfing is an extreme sport. It harnesses the power of the wind to pull you across the water. However, with modern safety systems and the right location, the risk can be mitigated almost entirely. Cartagena, specifically the La Boquilla area, is arguably one of the safest places in the world to learn. Here is a detailed breakdown of why, and how we handle safety at Marine Tech.

1. The Geography: Side-Onshore Wind

The most critical safety factor in kitesurfing is wind direction.

  • Offshore Wind (Dangerous): Blows from the land to the sea. If you crash or lose your board, you drift away from the coast.
  • Onshore Wind (Tricky): Blows directly onto the beach. This can push beginners onto the sand, causing accidents on land.
  • Side-Onshore (Perfect): Blows at a 45-degree angle towards the shore.

In Cartagena, specifically in La Boquilla, we enjoy Side-Onshore wind 95% of the time. This means that if you release your kite, break a line, or simply get tired, the wind and the waves will naturally push you back to the safe, sandy beach. You will never drift out to the open ocean here. This geographic "safety net" provides immense peace of mind for beginners.

2. Beach Hazards: What to Watch Out For

While the wind direction is perfect, no spot is without hazards. In Cartagena, the main obstacles are not sharks or rocks (we have neither in the kiting area), but rather man-made structures and other beach users.

The Groynes (Espolones): La Boquilla has stone breakwaters spaced out every kilometer or so. While they protect the beach from erosion, they are hard objects you do not want to hit.

Our Solution: At Marine Tech, we conduct our beginner lessons in the "open gaps" between these groynes. Furthermore, we use a support boat for many lessons to take students far upwind, away from any hard structures, giving them miles of open water to practice safely.

3. The Marine Tech Protocol

Safety isn't just about luck; it's about protocol. Drawing from my naval background, we have established a standard operating procedure (SOP) that every instructor must follow. This is what sets us apart from the "beach bum" schools.

A. Equipment Inspection

Before every session, we check the "Quick Release" systems. Modern kites have a "push-away" safety system that instantly kills the kite's power. We test this mechanism daily to ensure sand or salt hasn't jammed it. We also inspect lines for knots or fraying, as a snapped line can cause a kite to loop uncontrollably.

B. Radio Communication

Yelling instructions over the sound of wind and crashing waves is ineffective and stressful. All our students wear BbTalkin waterproof radio helmets. This allows the instructor to speak calmly into your ear, correcting your technique in real-time. This lowers anxiety and prevents panic.

C. Hydration & Physical State

Dehydration leads to cramps and poor decision-making. In the tropical sun of Cartagena, you lose water faster than you realize. We include mandatory hydration breaks in our lesson plans. We also assess the student's swimming ability before we even touch a kite.

4. Marine Traffic

Cartagena is a busy port city. However, the kitesurfing zone in La Boquilla is far removed from the commercial shipping lanes and the frantic jet-ski traffic of Bocagrande. The water here is designated for non-motorized sports (mostly fishermen and kitesurfers), making it a tranquil zone for learning.

Summary: Why We Are Safe

  • Natural Safety: Side-onshore winds drift you back to shore.
  • No Dangerous Marine Life: No sharks or stinging jellyfish in the kite zone.
  • Soft Bottom: Sandy bottom, no sharp coral reefs to cut your feet.
  • Naval Standards: Radio helmets, boat support, and rigorous gear checks.

Kitesurfing changes lives. It connects you with nature in a way few other sports can. But it demands respect. By choosing a school that prioritizes safety over profit, you ensure that your adrenaline rush remains a positive memory forever.
