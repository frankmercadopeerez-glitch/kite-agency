Top Water Sports in Cartagena (Beyond the Beach)

Marine Tech Team • 6 Min Read

Cartagena is more than just history; it is an aquatic playground. While Kitesurfing is the king of the windy season, there are plenty of other ways to enjoy the warm Caribbean waters. Here is our curated list of top activities for the active traveler.

1. Wing Foiling: The Future is Here

Wing Foiling is the fastest-growing water sport in the world, and for good reason. It combines the freedom of a foil board with a handheld wing. It requires less wind than kitesurfing and takes up less space.

Why try it? It’s incredibly silent and smooth. Once you are up on the foil, you feel like you are levitating. We offer tow-in lessons behind our boats to help you master the foil balance first.

2. Sunset Stand Up Paddle (SUP)

When the wind dies down in the late afternoon (usually around 5:30 PM), the ocean at La Boquilla turns into a mirror. This is the perfect time to rent a SUP board.

  • Difficulty: Very Easy. Suitable for all ages.
  • The Experience: You can paddle near the shore or explore the nearby mangrove tunnels for a nature-filled experience.
  • Our Standard: All Marine Tech rentals include a waterproof bag for your phone and hydration.

3. The Downwind Expedition (Advanced)

This isn't just a sport; it's a journey. We organize trips for groups of 5 or more skilled riders. We drive north, set up our kites, and ride downwind all the way to the Rosario Islands archipelago.

It is a full-day commitment costing roughly $300 USD per person, but it includes private transport, a support boat, and a premium lunch on an island. It is the ultimate bucket-list item for kiters.

4. Hydrofoil Tow-In

No wind? No problem. Learning to ride a hydrofoil board behind a boat is the best cross-training you can do for kite foiling or wing foiling. It builds the muscle memory needed to balance on the mast without the distraction of managing a kite or wing.
